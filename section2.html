Historical Background & Viewership

Historical Background

The NBA was first established in 1946 as the Basketball Association of America (BAA). In 1949, it merged with the NBL to become the modern NBA. Fifty years later, in 1996, the WNBA was founded; its inaugural season began in 1997 with eight teams. The WNBA was the first women’s professional basketball league in the U.S. to launch with full backing from an established men’s league.

Viewership

On average, the NBA draws about 1.6 million viewers per regular-season game, while the WNBA averages around 505,000. Playoffs and Finals amplify the gap: recent NBA Finals have drawn roughly 17.11 million viewers, compared to approximately 1.12 million for the WNBA Finals. Many argue this audience difference heavily influences league revenues and, downstream, salaries.
